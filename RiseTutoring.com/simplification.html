SIMPLIFICATION

Simplification is the process of reducing a mathematical expression to its simplest form. This involves combining like terms, reducing fractions, and performing basic arithmetic operations.

What is Simplification?

 Simplification involves making an expression easier to understand and work with by performing all possible operations and combining like terms.

Simplifying Expressions:

 1. **Combine Like Terms**: Terms that have the same variables and exponents.
 Example: 3x + 5x = 8x

 2. **Perform Arithmetic Operations**: Carry out any addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.
 Example: 4(3 + 2) = 4 × 5 = 20

 3. **Reduce Fractions**: Simplify fractions by dividing the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor.
 Example: 15/20 = 3/4

 4. **Use the Distributive Property**: a(b + c) = ab + ac
 Example: 2(x + 3) = 2x + 6

Steps for Simplification:

 1. **Identify and Combine Like Terms**:
   • Example: 2x + 3x = 5x

 2. **Use the Distributive Property if Necessary**:
   • Example: 3(2x + 4) = 6x + 12

 3. **Simplify Inside Parentheses**:
   • Example: 3(x + 2) + 4 = 3x + 6 + 4 = 3x + 10

 4. **Reduce Fractions to Lowest Terms**:
   • Example: 8/12 = 2/3

Simplifying Complex Expressions:

 For more complex expressions, follow these additional steps:

 1. **Simplify Exponents and Radicals**:
   • Example: √16 = 4

 2. **Perform Operations in the Correct Order (PEMDAS)**:
   • Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division (from left to right), Addition and Subtraction (from left to right).
   • Example: 3 + 2 × (8 - 5) ÷ 2 = 3 + 2 × 3 ÷ 2 = 3 + 6 ÷ 2 = 3 + 3 = 6

 3. **Factor where Possible**:
   • Example: x² + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3)

 4. **Combine All Simplified Parts**:
   • Ensure the expression is fully simplified.

Practice Problems:

1. Simplify 3x + 4x.
2. Simplify 2(3x + 4).
3. Simplify (2x + 3) + (4x - 1).
4. Simplify 10/15.
5. Simplify 3x² + 6x.

Tips and Tricks:

 • Always look for like terms to combine.
 • Perform operations in the correct order to avoid mistakes.
 • Practice regularly to become familiar with different types of simplification problems.
