FACTORISATION

Factorisation is a crucial concept in algebra that involves breaking down numbers or expressions into their constituent factors. This process is essential for simplifying equations, solving problems, and understanding the properties of numbers. Let's explore the different methods and types of factorisation:

Factorisation is the process of expressing a number or an algebraic expression as a product of its factors. Factors are numbers or expressions that multiply together to give the original number or expression.

Number Factorisation:

 12 = 2 × 2 × 3

To factorise a number, we break it down into its prime factors.

 Steps for Prime Factorisation:

 1. Divide the number by the smallest prime number (2, 3, 5, 7, etc.).
 2.Continue dividing the quotient by prime numbers until you reach 1.
 3.The prime factors are all the prime numbers you used in the divisions.

 Example: Factorise 60

  60 ÷ 2 = 30
  30 ÷ 2 = 15
  15 ÷ 3 = 5
  5 ÷ 5 = 1

  So, 60 = 2² × 3 × 5.

Common Factor method

Find the greatest common factor (GCF) of all terms and factor it out.

 Example: Factorise 6x²+9x

 •GCF of 6x²+9x is 3x.

 •6x²+9x = 3x(2x+3).

Difference of Squares

This applies to expressions of the form a² - b².

 Formula: a² - b² = (a-b)(a+b)

 Example: Factorise x²-16:

 (x-4)(x+4).

Trinomials

For quadratic expressions of the form ax² + bx + c, look for two numbers that multiply to ac and add to b.

 Example: Factorise x² + 5x + 6

 • Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5 (2 and 3).
 • x² + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3).

Grouping Method

This method is used when you have four or more terms.

 Example: Factorise x³ + 3x² + 2x + 6

 • Group terms: (x³ + 3x²) + (2x + 6).
 • Factor each group: x²(x + 3) + 2(x + 3).
 • Factor out the common binomial: (x + 3)(x² + 2).

Special Factorisation Formulas

 1. Perfect Square Trinomial:
   • a² + 2ab + b² = (a + b)²
   • a² - 2ab + b² = (a - b)²

 2. Sum and Difference of Cubes:
   • a³ + b³ = (a + b)(a² - ab + b²)
   • a³ - b³ = (a - b)(a² + ab + b²)

Practice Problems

1. Factorise 45.
2. Factorise 4x² - 9.
3. Factorise x² + 7x + 12.
4. Factorise x³ - 4x.
5. Factorise x² - 10x + 25.

Tips and Tricks

 • Always look for a common factor first.
 • Recognise special patterns like difference of squares or perfect square trinomials.
 • Practice regularly to become familiar with different types of factorisation problems.
