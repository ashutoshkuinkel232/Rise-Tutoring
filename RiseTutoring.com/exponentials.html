EXPONENTIALS

Exponentials involve numbers raised to a power. Understanding exponentials is crucial for working with large numbers, scientific notation, and various algebraic operations.

What are Exponentials?

 Exponentials refer to expressions where a number (the base) is raised to a power (the exponent).
 Example: 23 means 2 is the base and 3 is the exponent, which equals 2 × 2 × 2 = 8.

Properties of Exponents:

 1. **Product of Powers**: am × an = am+n
 Example: 23 × 22 = 23+2 = 25 = 32

 2. **Quotient of Powers**: am ÷ an = am-n
 Example: 25 ÷ 22 = 25-2 = 23 = 8

 3. **Power of a Power**: (am)n = am×n
 Example: (23)2 = 23×2 = 26 = 64

 4. **Power of a Product**: (ab)m = am × bm
 Example: (2 × 3)2 = 22 × 32 = 4 × 9 = 36

 5. **Power of a Quotient**: (a/b)m = am / bm
 Example: (4/2)2 = 42 / 22 = 16 / 4 = 4

 6. **Zero Exponent**: a0 = 1 (for any non-zero a)
 Example: 50 = 1

 7. **Negative Exponent**: a-n = 1/an
 Example: 2-3 = 1/23 = 1/8

Simplifying Exponential Expressions:

 1. **Combine Like Bases**:
   • Example: 23 × 24 = 23+4 = 27 = 128

 2. **Simplify Negative Exponents**:
   • Example: 3-2 = 1/32 = 1/9

 3. **Apply Power of a Power**:
   • Example: (x2)3 = x2×3 = x6

 4. **Use Power of a Product**:
   • Example: (2x)3 = 23 × x3 = 8x3

Special Exponential Cases:

 1. **Scientific Notation**:
   • Used to express very large or very small numbers.
   • Example: 5,000 = 5 × 103
   • Example: 0.0004 = 4 × 10-4

 2. **Exponential Growth and Decay**:
   • Exponential growth: y = a(1 + r)t
   • Exponential decay: y = a(1 - r)t

 3. **Logarithms (inverse of exponentials)**:
   • loga(ax) = x
   • aloga(x) = x

Practice Problems:

1. Simplify 23 × 24.
2. Simplify (32)3.
3. Simplify 50.
4. Simplify 10-2.
5. Write 6,300 in scientific notation.

Tips and Tricks:

 • Remember the basic properties of exponents.
 • Convert negative exponents to positive by taking the reciprocal.
 • Use scientific notation for very large or small numbers to simplify calculations.
