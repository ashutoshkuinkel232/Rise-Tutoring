ARITHMETICS

Basic arithmetic is the foundation of all mathematics. It includes the fundamental operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Mastering these operations is essential for solving everyday math problems.

What is Arithmetic?

 Arithmetic is the branch of mathematics dealing with numbers and the basic operations: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Addition:

 Addition is combining two or more numbers to get a sum.
 Example: 3 + 5 = 8

 Properties of Addition:
   • Commutative Property: a + b = b + a
   • Associative Property: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
   • Identity Property: a + 0 = a

Subtraction:

 Subtraction is finding the difference between two numbers.
 Example: 8 - 5 = 3

 Properties of Subtraction:
   • Subtraction is not commutative: a - b ≠ b - a
   • Subtraction is not associative: (a - b) - c ≠ a - (b - c)

Multiplication:

 Multiplication is combining equal groups to find a product.
 Example: 4 × 3 = 12

 Properties of Multiplication:
   • Commutative Property: a × b = b × a
   • Associative Property: (a × b) × c = a × (b × c)
   • Identity Property: a × 1 = a
   • Zero Property: a × 0 = 0

Division:

 Division is splitting a number into equal parts.
 Example: 12 ÷ 4 = 3

 Properties of Division:
   • Division by 1: a ÷ 1 = a
   • Division by itself: a ÷ a = 1 (for a ≠ 0)
   • Division by zero is undefined.

Order of Operations:

 To solve arithmetic problems accurately, follow the order of operations:
   • Parentheses ( )
   • Exponents ^
   • Multiplication × and Division ÷ (from left to right)
   • Addition + and Subtraction - (from left to right)

 Example: 3 + 2 × (8 - 5) ÷ 2 = 3 + 2 × 3 ÷ 2 = 3 + 6 ÷ 2 = 3 + 3 = 6
